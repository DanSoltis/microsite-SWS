8:30 - 10:00 am

Opening & Keynote Ben Grumbles, Maryland's Secretary of the Environment
Moderator: Harry Stark
Partnerships, resiliency and economic growth. Urban and suburban storm water runoff solutions go beyond fixing the important environmental, infrastructure and public health impacts because they also can result in the added co-benefits of greener, more resilient communities and a restoration-focused economy supporting economic growth. NPDES MS4 regulatory frameworks must be designed to allow tailored solutions to local problems, to foster innovation through pooled research opportunities and build partnerships through tools such as water quality trading. Secretary Grumbles will speak about national trends in storm water permitting, as well as specifically his efforts in Maryland.

8:30 - 9:30 am

Panel Discussion
Moderators: Harry Stark & Derek Berg, StormTank
Please join us for a storm water panel discussion, moderated by the Stormwater Equipment Manufacturers Association (SWEMA), comprised of site designers, governmental regulators, manufacturers, and environmental advocates comparing the use of green infrastructure practices to proprietary storm water treatment devices within urban, suburban and retrofit applications. The panel will discuss a range of topics such as: design applicability/implementation, performance verification, constructability and maintenance considerations.
