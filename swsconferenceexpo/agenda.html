8:30 - 10:00 a.m. Opening & Keynote
Ben Grumbles, Maryland's Secretary of the Environment
 Partnerships, resiliency and economic growth. Urban and suburban storm water runoff solutions go beyond fixing the important environmental, infrastructure and public health impacts because they also can result in the added co-benefits of greener, more resilient communities and a restoration-focused economy supporting economic growth. NPDES MS4 regulatory frameworks must be designed to allow tailored solutions to local problems, to foster innovation through pooled research opportunities and build partnerships through tools such as water quality trading. Secretary Grumbles will speak about national trends in storm water permitting, as well as specifically his efforts in Maryland.
